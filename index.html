ABOUT ME!

I am a robotics and control systems engineer working at the intersection of autonomy, multi-robot coordination, reinforcement learning, and machine perception. My work explores how classical control theory and modern machine learning combine to build interpretable and reliable autonomous systems. In addition, I also design lightweight experimental platforms and simulation environments to study navigation, coordination, and decision-making in robotic systems. Beyond formal showcase work, I enjoy experimenting with autonomous agents in unconventional environments, from ultra-lightweight drone simulators to reinforcement-learning bots in classic open-source game engines such as Doom and Quake III Arena. These explorations often serve as experimental sandboxes for testing ideas in autonomy, control, and learning.

If you are working on interesting ideas in robotics, autonomy, or intelligent systems, or enjoy experimenting with unconventional autonomy work, feel free to reach out (see contact links at the bottom of the page).

PPO-driven Swarm Control


A hybrid multi-agent control framework combining PPO-based local navigation with classical coordination to produce safe, coherent, and interpretable swarm behavior.

• PPO policy trained on local 128×128 observation crops.
• Learned policy used strictly as local navigation primitive.
• Artificial potential fields for inter-agent spacing and coverage shaping.
• Repulsion layer eliminates unsafe proximity and clustering.
• Graph-based consensus for directional coherence across agents.
• Direction consensus operates on velocity vectors, not positions.
• CRN-inspired stochastic role switching (Explorer, Surveyor, Defender, Idle).
• Controlled hybrid composition instead of end-to-end multi-agent RL.
• Quantitative evaluation: coverage, safety, spacing, redundancy metrics.
• Demonstrates structured gains in safety and coordination over naive PPO.

Reflectivity-Augmented LiDAR Scene Understanding for Robotic Perception


A LiDAR perception analysis framework studying whether reflectivity-inspired augmentation of raw intensity improves semantic structure and temporal stability.

• Raw LiDAR intensity analyzed as entangled signal (range + geometry effects).
• Pseudo-reflectivity constructed via intensity × range coupling.
• Treated as controlled transformation, not true reflectivity recovery.
• Class-wise distribution analysis across semantic categories.
• Evaluates separability improvements over raw intensity baseline.
• Temporal stability studied across short-horizon motion windows.
• Multi-frame consistency analysis of signal behavior.
• SemanticKITTI dataset used for structured evaluation.
• Visualization + statistical inspection pipeline.
• Establishes limited but measurable structural advantage conditions.

MicroUAV-2D


An ultra-lightweight UAV simulation environment designed for rapid prototyping of vision-driven autonomy and control systems.

• Downward-facing UAV model over overhead imagery.
• Local field-of-view observation extraction (perception-driven control).
• Clean perception → action interface for navigation policies.
• Deterministic simulation behavior with stable border handling.
• Minimal implementation using NumPy + OpenCV only.
• ~17 KB simulation core (extremely low overhead).
• No dependency on ROS, Gazebo, or physics engines.
• Real-time visualization and rapid iteration capability.
• Used as base environment for RL and swarm experiments.
• Designed for fast experimentation and reproducible autonomy research.
